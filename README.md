This repository contains the demo code for the paper ***A Matrix-Analytic Characterization of Occlusion in Imaging Inverse Problems*** by Arina Oberoi.

Abstract. This paper gives a matrix-analytic characterization of when occlusion in imaging inverse problems is correctable versus when it is not. The central claim is that the failure is algebraic rather than statistical: when masking removes measurement directions, the corresponding image-space direction may enter the null space of the masked normal matrix B = A∗M∗MA, making it such that no reconstruction method that depends only on a matrix function of B can recover that direction from the retained data, regardless of regularization. The framework developed here uses four tools of matrix analysis - spectral calculus, condition numbers, Fr'echet derivatives, and the L'owner partial order - to establish a mathematical basis for distinguishing true absence from structural inability to observe. The framework is finally illustrated through a numerical experiment on an ultrasound with confirmed acoustic shadowing, where the baseline reconstruction diverges by a factor of 10^12 while the matrix analytic approach correctly identifies 45 of 64 image directions as structurally unobservable (indicating that, within the modeled acquisition geometry, any negative finding cannot be interpreted as evidence of observability) and reduces reconstruction error from ∥xˆ − x∥ ≈ 7.5 × 1012 to ∥xˆ − x∥ ≈ 1.07 in the 19 directions that remain observable.
